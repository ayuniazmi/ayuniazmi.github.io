# Meridian University Student Handbook

*A fictional handbook written as a stand-in corpus for this evaluation framework — not a real institution's policy document.*

## Admissions and Enrollment

Meridian University admits students for the Fall and Spring semesters. Applications for the Fall semester are due by March 1, and applications for the Spring semester are due by October 1. Prospective undergraduate students must submit a completed application form, official secondary school transcripts, two letters of recommendation, and a personal statement of no more than 650 words. Transfer students must additionally submit transcripts from all previously attended post-secondary institutions. Admitted students must confirm enrollment and pay a non-refundable deposit of $250 within 30 days of receiving an offer, or the offer is automatically withdrawn. International applicants must submit proof of English proficiency via TOEFL (minimum score 90) or IELTS (minimum score 6.5) unless their prior education was conducted entirely in English.

## Academic Calendar

The academic year is divided into two main semesters, Fall and Spring, plus an optional Summer term. The Fall semester begins the last week of August and ends in mid-December, including a one-week Thanksgiving recess. The Spring semester begins in mid-January and ends in early May, including a one-week Spring Break in March. Final examinations are held during the last week of each semester. The Summer term runs for eight weeks in June and July and is optional; not all courses are offered during the Summer term. The last day to add or drop a course without penalty is the end of the second week of classes in the Fall and Spring semesters, and the end of the first week in the Summer term.

## Grading and Assessment

Meridian University uses a 4.0 grade point scale. Letter grades of A, A-, B+, B, B-, C+, C, C-, D+, D, and F are recorded on the official transcript, along with a grade of W for an authorized withdrawal. A grade of C- or above is required to satisfy prerequisite requirements in a student's declared major. Students may request a grade appeal within 30 days of a final grade being posted; appeals must be submitted in writing to the instructor's department chair and are reviewed by a three-member faculty committee. Incomplete grades (marked "I") may be assigned at an instructor's discretion when a student has substantially completed coursework but is unable to finish due to documented illness or emergency; incomplete work must be finished within one semester or the grade converts automatically to an F.

## Attendance Policy

Regular class attendance is expected of all students. Individual instructors set their own attendance policies within their syllabi, and these policies are binding once published. For students receiving federal financial aid, attendance is monitored during the first three weeks of each semester to comply with federal reporting requirements; a student who never attends a course in which they are enrolled may have that course removed from their financial aid calculation. Excused absences are granted for documented medical emergencies, required religious observances, and university-sanctioned activities such as varsity athletics; students must notify instructors in advance where possible. Excessive unexcused absences, as defined by the instructor's syllabus, may result in a reduction of the final course grade or administrative withdrawal from the course.

## Financial Aid and Tuition

Tuition and fees are billed once per semester and are due no later than the first day of classes. A payment plan spreading tuition across four monthly installments is available to all students who apply before the semester billing deadline. Students seeking need-based financial aid must file the FAFSA (or the university's institutional aid application for international students) by the priority deadline of February 15 for the following academic year; applications received after this date are considered on a funds-available basis only. Merit scholarships awarded at admission are renewable each year provided the student maintains a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0 and full-time enrollment status. Students who withdraw from the university during a semester are subject to a prorated tuition refund schedule, with no refund issued after the 60% point of the semester has passed.

## Code of Conduct

All students are expected to uphold the university's Honor Code, which prohibits cheating, plagiarism, fabrication of data or sources, and unauthorized collaboration on individual assignments. Suspected violations are reported to the Office of Student Conduct and investigated by a hearing panel composed of faculty and student representatives. Sanctions for a first offense typically range from a failing grade on the assignment to a failing grade in the course, depending on severity; repeat offenses may result in suspension or expulsion. Students have the right to a hearing, to review the evidence against them, and to appeal a finding to the Dean of Students within 14 days of the decision. Harassment, discrimination, and hazing are separately prohibited under the university's Student Conduct Code and are handled by the Office of Equity and Title IX Compliance rather than the Honor Code process.

## Student Support Services

The university provides free tutoring for undergraduate students through the Academic Success Center, located in the main library, with both drop-in hours and scheduled appointments available. Students with documented disabilities may register with the Office of Accessibility Services to receive approved accommodations, such as extended test time or note-taking support; accommodations are not applied retroactively and must be arranged each semester. Counseling and mental health services are available at no charge to enrolled students through the Student Wellness Center, which also operates a 24-hour crisis line. Career Services offers resume review, mock interviews, and an on-campus recruiting program each Fall and Spring semester.

## Graduation Requirements

To graduate, undergraduate students must complete a minimum of 120 credit hours, satisfy all general education requirements, complete the requirements of at least one declared major with a cumulative GPA of 2.0 or higher in that major, and achieve an overall cumulative GPA of at least 2.0. Students must file an Application for Graduation with the Registrar's Office no later than the start of their final semester. A degree audit is conducted by the Registrar to confirm all requirements are met before a degree is conferred. Students who complete all requirements except for a small number of remaining credit hours may petition to participate in the graduation ceremony under an approved "walk-only" status, though the degree itself is not conferred until all requirements are complete.
