About

Eight years in a classroom taught me more about systems than any course did.

I started as an educator with the Ministry of Education, spending close to eight years leading EdTech adoption across 80+ teachers and running a $50,000 annual overseas learning programme with zero safety incidents. Teaching is, underneath everything, a systems problem: you're constantly translating between what people need, what's actually possible, and how to get a room full of different stakeholders moving in the same direction.

While teaching full-time, I completed a degree in Information Technology and Business, which became my way into tech. A short stint as a Data Privacy Officer Intern at GXS Bank gave me a close look at how governance, risk, and AI systems collide in a regulated environment. From there I joined Google as a Customer Engineer Trainee on the Workspace and Gemini team, designing and demoing AI solutions for enterprise customers like DBS and SUSS, and building the internal tooling that other engineers used to sell those solutions.

Today I'm a Business Analyst at NCS, working across three concurrent engagements — most notably a $10M government AI Knowledge Management tender, where I've mapped user stories across five stakeholder groups and surfaced the legacy-system risks (a 20-year-old Lotus Notes archive, among other things) that shaped how the solution was ultimately pitched.

My long-term goal is to become a Solutions Architect or Forward Deployed Engineer — someone who sits at the intersection of a customer's real, messy constraints and what engineering can actually build. This site is where I'm documenting that transition, in the open, as it happens.
